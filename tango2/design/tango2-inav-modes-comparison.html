Claude proposal

Rationale summary

  • Left hand = "safety hand": ARM on SA, NAV (LOITER/RTH) on SB. Nav can be reached without leaving the right stick.
  • Right hand = "flying hand": flight mode on SC, LAUNCH on SD.
  • ANGLE at LOW of flight-mode switch because panic flinch = pull toward body.
  • RTH at HIGH of nav switch (opposite-end of LOITER).

Unique calls

  • Finger layout groups safety (ARM+NAV) on left hand.
  • Suggests alternative: temporarily swap LAUNCH↔AUTO TUNE on SD for tuning sessions, skip the logical switch entirely.

ChatGPT proposal

Rationale summary

  • Separates ARM (right, SD) from LAUNCH (left, SA) — can't accidentally hit both with one hand.
  • RTH at DOWN of SC because "panic flip to extreme" is natural and distinct.
  • LOITER at UP of SC.
  • ACRO = SB centered (no aux active).

aux 4 10  2 1800 2100  # RTH     SC high
aux 5 36  3 1800 2100  # LAUNCH  SA high
aux 6 21  4 1800 2100  # TUNE    LS1→CH9
aux 7 13  5 1800 2100  # BEEPER  SE

Unique calls

  • Recommends voice callouts for AUTO TUNE on/off — "you really don't want to forget it's active".
  • Flags NAV LAUNCH re-entry in flight as the biggest operational gotcha.
  • Notes PREARM trade-off explicitly: reclaim SA if you later want it more than dedicated launch.

Gemini proposal

Rationale summary

  • Same channel map as ChatGPT (ARM=SD, MODE=SB, NAV=SC, LAUNCH=SA).
  • RTH at DOWN of SC — "strongest recovery mode on end-stop under stress".
  • LOITER at UP of SC.
  • Emphasises isolation: ARM separated from launch/tune/flight-mode.

Aux block

aux 0  0  0 1800 2100  # ARM     SD
aux 1  1  1  900 1300  # ANGLE   SB low
aux 2 12  1 1700 2100  # MANUAL  SB high
aux 3 10  2  900 1300  # RTH     SC low
aux 4 11  2 1700 2100  # POSHOLD SC high
aux 5 36  3 1700 2100  # LAUNCH  SA
aux 6 13  4 1700 2100  # BEEPER  SE
aux 7 21  5 1700 2100  # TUNE    SF toggle

Unique calls

  • Widest mid-position dead zones (uses 1700 not 1800 as the high-band start).
  • Explicit per-plane AIRMODE note (ON for Baloo/Dolphin, OFF for Swordfish — preserve builder intent).
  • Flags SB HIGH (MANUAL) as "dangerous for beginners" — worth a warning to self.

Where they converge

  • ANGLE at LOW of the mode switch — all three.
  • BEEPER on SE, AUTO TUNE on SF via a sticky logical switch — all three. Identical approach to the momentary-button problem.
  • LAUNCH on a dedicated 2-position switch — all three.
  • Skip PREARM in the baseline — all three.
  • Single identical map across all three planes — all three.

Where they differ

  • Claude puts ARM on left hand, LAUNCH on right hand. ChatGPT and Gemini do the opposite (ARM right, LAUNCH left). Neither is obviously wrong; it's about which side you trust for the "live motor" flick.
  • Claude puts RTH at HIGH of nav switch (push forward for emergency). ChatGPT and Gemini put RTH at LOW (pull toward you). ChatGPT frames this as "panic flip to extreme = natural with right hand", Gemini as "end-stop = strongest mode". Claude frames HIGH as "opposite to LOITER at LOW, so muscle memory can't confuse them". Depends on which gesture you find more instinctive under stress.
  • Claude offers a non-LS alternative for AUTO TUNE — temporary swap with LAUNCH on SD rather than configuring a sticky logical switch. Simpler Tango-side.
  • ChatGPT and Gemini converged on essentially the same channel map, and on the "ARM and LAUNCH on opposite grips" philosophy.

Suggested decision path

  • If you already have muscle memory for ARM on the right grip, go with ChatGPT/Gemini — they're essentially the same.
  • If you want LAUNCH and ARM on opposite hands as a safety isolation rule, ChatGPT/Gemini.
  • If you want RTH reachable by pushing forward (same direction as "emergency, go away"), Claude.
  • If you want to skip EdgeTX logical-switch config entirely during initial tuning, Claude's alternative (swap LAUNCH↔AUTO TUNE on a single switch when tuning).
  • After the first tuning flights you can retune any of this — the important thing is to commit to ONE of these three and memorise it.
